Project WORK

Project WORK (Winning = Opportunities, Resources, and Knowledge) is a transformative workforce development initiative of the Bobby Byars Foundation designed to equip individuals with the tools they need to achieve sustainable employment and long-term success.

Project WORK with STEM

Project WORK with STEM is an innovative workforce and education initiative of the Bobby Byars Foundation designed to prepare youth and emerging professionals for high-demand careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). The program provides hands-on learning experiences, mentorship, career exploration, and industry-aligned training that bridge the gap between education and employment.

Through strategic partnerships, skill-building workshops, and access to essential resources, Project WORK with STEM empowers participants to develop technical competencies, critical thinking skills, and career readiness. By combining academic enrichment with real-world exposure, the program creates pathways to sustainable careers, economic mobility, and long-term success—ensuring that opportunity, knowledge, and resources translate into winning futures.

Who Is Eligible

Project WORK is open to students in middle school and high school who are interested in making an impact in their community while developing important skills.
